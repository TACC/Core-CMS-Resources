Meet the
Fellows

The Frontera Computational Science Fellowship provides a year-long opportunity for graduate students to work on the most powerful academic supercomputer in the world. To learn more about the Fellows, please visit the Frontera Portal.

  • Maureen Kitheka

    State University of New York at Binghamton Computational Chemistry Frontera is enabling me to investigate questions about the mechanism of charge transport in crystalline quinones, which are becoming increasingly important as electrode materials in storage devices.

  • Danat Issa

    Northwestern University Astrophysics I‘m tackling a long-standing problem of what happens in the aftermath of a neutron star colliding with another neutron star. Using Frontera, my goal is to build a first-principle model of the aftermath of neutron star mergers.

  • Dubem Okafor

    University of Iowa Elastoplastic Wave
    Propagation
    in Impacting Media     I use mathematical modeling to understand the physics governing the behavior of complex physical processes involved in materials under extreme conditions. This opportunity to work on Frontera is enabling me to run my own simulations independently.

  • Jeane Camelo

    University of Central Florida Coastal Engineering Weather forecasts and storm surge affect the uncertainty in prediction and involve running several historical and synthetic storm scenarios. Having access to Frontera is allowing me to get results in a timely manner, which is crucial to evacuation operations.

  • Gabriele Bozzola

    University of Arizona Physics & Astrophysics As a Frontera fellow, I‘m extending existing codes to perform ray tracing in general-relativistic simulations to interpret present and future astronomical observations. I‘ll make my contribution available to the community to help discover accreting binary black holes.
